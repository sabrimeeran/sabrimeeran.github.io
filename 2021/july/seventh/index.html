The question(s)

What rate of inflation should we prepare for in the future? And how?

Economics is nothing if not unpredictable! What if we expect 5-year 3% inflation, but instead inflation is as high as 4% or 5%? Should we not at least prepare for this possibility? We should start by asking how this may occur - what's different about today that could cause prolonged higher inflation?

The components

We can look at the Mean Trimmed PCE to find inflation data for individual components. I found similar data for CPI in the UK, which will be used later.

We should first look at components where inflation >10%. It's a startlingly large percentage of the components - 17.84% - that are experiencing >10% inflation! And it appears these components are contributing 23.4 percentage points to inflation (!)

One possibility is that accelerating inflation encourages supermarkets to ration essential items, which is similar to events during the toilet roll shortages and panic buying in early 2020. Online auction prices could be four to six times the retail price according to the Guardian. In this way, quantity control could reduce component price inflation, resulting in a lower CPI statistic.
